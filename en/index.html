Introduction to Deep Learning

Introduction to Deep Learning explores the subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with substantial depth to make predictions and decisions across diverse domains such as computer vision, natural language processing, and audio recognition. This introduction covers the fundamentals of deep learning architectures, such as convolutional and recurrent neural networks, emphasizing the importance of large data sets and substantial computing power. Understanding these basics allows learners to appreciate how deep learning models automatically learn high-level abstractions from data, providing a foundation for advancing into more complex, practical applications in AI.

01.00PM - 03.00PM

Natural Learning Processing

Natural Learning Processing likely refers to a typographical error for "Natural Language Processing" (NLP), a branch of artificial intelligence focused on the interaction between computers and humans through natural language. The field encompasses tasks such as speech recognition, language translation, and sentiment analysis. NLP involves the application of algorithms to identify and extract the natural language rules, enabling the conversion of unstructured language data into a form that computers can understand. This foundational aspect of AI is crucial for developing applications that require human-like interaction capabilities.

02.00PM - 04.10PM

Building and Preparing Datasets for Machine Learning

Building and Preparing Datasets for Machine Learning involves a critical phase of collecting, cleaning, and organizing data to ensure the quality and effectiveness of machine learning models. The process starts with dataset construction—gathering data from various sources and ensuring it is relevant to the problem at hand. Data cleaning then follows, which involves handling missing values, removing duplicates, and correcting errors. Finally, data is often transformed and normalized to fit the machine learning algorithms' requirements, enhancing model accuracy and efficiency. This preparation stage is foundational, as the quality of data directly influences the predictive capabilities and success of machine learning projects.

Large Language Models delves into the capabilities and construction of advanced AI systems designed to understand, generate, and interact using human language. These models, built on deep learning architectures, can process vast amounts of text data, enabling them to perform tasks such as translation, summarization, and question answering with high proficiency. The presentation also explores the ethical considerations and technological challenges associated with training and deploying these powerful tools, providing a well-rounded overview of their impact and potential in various industries.

01.00PM - 03.00PM

Generative A.I with transformers

Generative A.I with Transformers explores the specialized use of transformer models, a type of deep learning architecture that excels in generating human-like text based on the input it receives. This session examines how transformers have revolutionized fields such as natural language processing by enabling more accurate and context-aware text generation. Participants will learn about the underlying mechanisms of transformer models, including self-attention mechanisms that allow the model to weigh the importance of different words in a sentence, regardless of their positional distance.
